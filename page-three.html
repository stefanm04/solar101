Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite. Its surface is covered with craters, plains, and mountains, and it is tidally locked to Earth, always showing the same side. The Moon influences Earth's tides and helps stabilize its axial tilt. It has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature variations.

Distance from Earth 384,400km
Diameter 3,475 km
Temperature 121°C to -133°C
Orbital Period 27.3 days

Phobos

Phobos is the larger of Mars' two moons. It has an irregular shape and is covered in craters, with grooves and ridges caused by tidal forces from Mars. Phobos orbits very close to Mars and is slowly moving closer to the planet, which could eventually lead to it breaking apart or colliding with Mars.

Distance to Mars 6,000km
Diameter 22.5km
Temperature -4°C to -112°C
Orbit Period ~7.7 hours

Ganymede

Ganymede is Jupiter's largest moon and the largest in the solar system. It has a rocky core, icy surface, a subsurface ocean, and is the only moon with its own magnetic field. Its terrain combines craters with grooved regions.

Distance to Jupiter 1.07 million km
Diameter 5,262km
Temperature -183°C to -113°C
Orbit Period ~7 days

Callisto

Callisto is Jupiter's second-largest moon, known for its heavily cratered and ancient surface, the oldest in the solar system. Beneath its icy crust, it may hold a subsurface ocean, making it a potential candidate for harboring life.

Distance to Jupiter 1.84 million km
Diameter 4,821km
Temperature -139°C
Orbit Period 17 days

Europa

Europa is one of Jupiter's major moons, with a smooth, icy surface crisscrossed by cracks and ridges. Beneath its ice shell lies a vast subsurface ocean, potentially harboring conditions suitable for life.

Distance to Jupiter 671,000 km
Diameter 3,122km
Temperature -134°C to -223°C
Orbit Period 3.5 days

Titan

Titan is Saturn's largest moon, with a thick atmosphere of nitrogen and methane. Its surface features rivers, lakes, and seas of liquid methane and ethane, making it one of the most Earth-like bodies in the solar system.

Distance to Saturn 1.2 milllion km
Diameter 5,150km
Temperature -179°C
Orbital Period 16 days

Titania

Titania is Uranus’s largest moon, with a surface marked by deep canyons and impact craters. Its icy crust may conceal a subsurface ocean, and past tectonic activity suggests it once experienced significant geological changes.

Distance to Uranus 435,840 milllion km
Diameter 1,577km
Temperature -203°C
Orbital Period 8.7 days

Triton

Triton is Neptune's largest moon, with a retrograde orbit suggesting it was captured. Its surface is covered in frozen nitrogen, and it features geysers that eject nitrogen gas, indicating ongoing geological activity.

Distance to Neptune 354,800 km
Diameter 2,707km
Temperature -235°C
Orbital Period ~6 days
