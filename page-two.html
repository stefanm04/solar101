Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. Despite its proximity to the Sun, it has extreme temperature fluctuations due to the lack of a significant atmosphere. Its surface is covered in craters and it experiences long periods of daylight and night.

Diameter 4,879 km
Temperature 430°C to -180°C
Day Length: ~59 Earth days
Orbit Period: 88 Earth days
Moons: 0

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. However, its thick atmosphere causes extreme heat and pressure on the surface, making it the hottest planet in our Solar System.

Diameter 12,104 km
Temperature 465°C
Day Length: ~243 Earth days
Orbit Period: 225 Earth days
Moons: 0
