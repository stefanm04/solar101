Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in the Solar System. Despite its proximity to the Sun, it has extreme temperature fluctuations due to the lack of a significant atmosphere. Its surface is covered in craters and it experiences long periods of daylight and night.

Diameter 4,879 km
Temperature 430°C to -180°C
Day Length 59 days
Orbit Period 88 days
Moons 0

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. However, its thick atmosphere causes extreme heat and pressure on the surface, making it the hottest planet in our Solar System.

Diameter 12,104 km
Temperature 465°C
Day Length 243 days
Orbit Period 225 days
Moons 0

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only known planet to support life, thanks to its atmosphere, water, and stable climate. It has a diverse surface with oceans, continents, and a wide range of ecosystems, making it unique in the Solar System.

Diameter 12,756 km
Temperature 15°C
Day Length 1 day
Orbit Period 365 days
Moons 1

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance. Mars has a diverse landscape with vast plains, towering volcanoes, and deep valleys, making it a focal point for scientific exploration in the search for past life and potential human colonization.

Diameter 6,779 km
Temperature -125°C to 20°C
Day Length ~1 day
Orbit Period 687 days
Moons 2

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and is the largest planet in our Solar System. Known for its distinctive Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has raged for centuries, Jupiter is a gas giant primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.

Diameter 139,820 km
Temperature -145°C to 24,000°C
Day Length ~10 hours
Orbit Period 12 years
Moons 95

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is known for its stunning system of rings, which are made up of ice and rock particles. As a gas giant, Saturn is primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and its atmosphere features bands of clouds that create dramatic weather patterns.

Diameter 120,500 km
Temperature -178°C
Day Length 10.7 hours
Orbit Period 29.4 years
Moons 146

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and an ice giant, known for its extreme tilt, with its axis rotated almost 90 degrees. It has a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere and is colder than most other planets. Uranus also has faint rings and numerous moons.

Diameter 51,118 km
Temperature -195°C
Day Length ~17 hours
Orbit Period 84 years
Moons 28

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun, known for its deep blue color and strong winds. It is an ice giant, primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and water, ammonia, and methane ices. Neptune has a dynamic atmosphere with large storms, including the Great Dark Spot, and is also home to a number of moons and faint rings.

Diameter 49,244 km
Temperature -214°C
Day Length ~16 hours
Orbit Period 165 years
Moons 16
